# Beast King — Manga Idea

## Pitch (3 lines)
Humans live in a dinosaur-dominated world where survival depends on elite protectors called Beast Kings.
A seemingly ordinary boy awakens a rare multi-soul ability during an apex predator attack, setting him on a path no one expected.
In a world where power comes from ancient pacts, the line between protector and predator is everything.

## Protagonists (top 5)

1. **MC (Beast King candidate)** — Village boy, appears 1-soul, secretly 3-soul. Wants to protect, not dominate. His arc: mastering multiple voices inside without losing himself.
2. **The Shaman** — Keeper of pact knowledge. Strategic asset. As shamans disappear, humanity loses its ability to produce new protectors — making her survival critical.
3. **The Rival** — A 2-soul warrior from a competing village with a different rite style. Starts as enemy, becomes reluctant ally.
4. **The Devourer** — Mirror villain. Chose carnivore pact + soul weaving. Became the apex predator he feared. What the MC could become if he picks domination over cooperation.
5. **The Seam King** — Hidden leader of the Weavers. Wants to manufacture a 7-soul vessel to end humanity's prey status forever. Final antagonist.

## Theme
**Becoming a predator to protect prey — and the identity cost that comes with it.**
Secondary: cooperation vs domination (earned power vs stolen power).

## Plot Twist
The world is not what it seems. Ruins, myths, and "cycle" hints gradually reveal that dinosaurs didn't simply survive alongside humans — the entire world may have been reset, engineered, or is running as a survival-pressure simulation. The protagonist's rare 3-soul may be a deliberate design, not an accident.

## Storyline (engine)
**Wall Expansion + Rite Hunt** (dual engine):
- Expand safe human zones → new biomes, stronger dinosaurs, rival village politics
- Recover lost pact rites, protect shamans → dungeon ruins, artifact cores, urgency escalates

### Arc roadmap

**Early arcs (survival + training)**
1. Night of Teeth — village disaster, MC's vow established
2. Shaman Trial — first pact, first rival introduced
3. First Hunt — mid-tier predator boss fight
4. Stampede War — herd + predator chaos, team tactics

**Mid arcs (politics + variety)**
5. Storm Village Alliance — team-up then betrayal
6. Nesting Grounds — high-stakes expedition
7. Volcano Season — environmental arc, visibility/poison/scarcity
8. Relic Ruin Dive — introduce weird old-world powers

**Reveal arcs (escalation)**
9. Stitched Soldier Incident — first Weaver reveal
10. Shaman Kidnap — urgency spikes, no new hunters possible
11. Devourer Hunt — mirror villain appears
12. Third Soul Awakening — major power jump

**Late arcs (endgame)**
13. Seam King War — Weavers vs all villages
14. Beast King Trial — hero must win without corruption
15. World Truth Reveal — reboot / terraform / simulation confirmed

## Power System Summary

### Souls
- 1-soul: most humans
- 2-soul: rare "Wall-class" (born, earned by rite, or forcibly woven)
- 3-soul: mythic — MC's hidden third awakens mid-series
- 7-soul: legend ceiling (endgame lore only)

### Aspects (pact types)
Beast / Nature / Material / Relic / Spirit / Human (forbidden)

### Pact Slots
- Most people: 1 slot
- Elite: 2–3 slots
- Beast King: 5 slots (mythic ceiling)
- Mixing creates unique combos (e.g. Beast:Scale + Nature:Ash = Ash Scale Mantle)

### Fusion modes
- 2-soul → Mantle form (seconds → minutes with training)
- 3-soul → Crown form (late series)
- Cost: time limit, afterburn crash, identity drift risk

## What makes it original
- Dinosaurs are not villains — humans are the real danger through stolen power
- Power is earned through pact/compatibility/vows, not random gifts
- Multi-soul is both evolution AND taboo technology (born vs manufactured)
- Shamans are strategic assets — protecting them drives entire arc categories
- Core mixing creates combinatorial creativity within clean constraints
